About the Workshop

The Machine Vision for Climate Change (MVCC) workshop at BMVC 2025 focuses on the intersection of computer vision and climate change mitigation, adaptation, and sustainability. The workshop aims to foster discussions and research on the use of cutting-edge vision technologies—such as deep learning, satellite imagery, UAVs, and remote sensing—for addressing climate and environmental challenges. We also invite critical perspectives on the environmental impact of large vision models themselves, including energy efficiency, ethics, and responsible deployment.

Workshop Topics

  • Sustainable Built Environment
    • Transport, Infrastructure, and Mobility
    • Urban Design and Planning for Sustainable Cities
    • Power and Energy, including Renewable Sources
  • Ecosystems and Natural Living Environment
    • Forestry and Land Use
    • Biodiversity and Automated Wildlife Monitoring
    • Remote Sensing Techniques for Climate Monitoring
    • Precision Agriculture, Crop Monitoring, Yield Prediction
    • Satellite Imagery Analysis for Deforestation and Land Use
  • Climate and Weather Events
    • Air Quality
    • Natural Disaster Management and Relief
    • Earth Observation Monitoring
    • Extreme Weather Events
    • Climate Change and Public Health
  • Vision Models Contributing to Climate Change
    • Ethics, Bias, and Accountability in Climate-related Vision Models
    • Lightweight Vision Models for Edge Deployment (e.g., Drones)
    • Improving Energy Utilisation for Vision Model Training
    • Interpretable AI and Explainability in Environmental Vision Systems
